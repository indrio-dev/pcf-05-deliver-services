## CROSS-REGION NOTES

### Shipping & Regulations

**Michigan Wine Law (2005)**
- Direct shipping: Permitted from wineries to consumers
- Retailer shipping: Strictly prohibited
- Rights: Tasting room sales, direct consumer shipping, direct sales to licensed retailers/restaurants

**Wisconsin Wine Law (2025)**
- Effective: January 1, 2025 (Senate Bill 616)
- Permit Required: Fulfillment house permit ($100 annual) for each location
- Direct Shipping: Up to 12 cases/year to consumers
- Retailer Shipping: Strictly prohibited
- Labeling: Must include fulfillment house name, address, permit number

**Ohio Wine Law**
- Lake Erie AVA: Multi-state (NY, PA, OH)
- Shipping: Contact individual wineries for policies

## SEASONAL AVAILABILITY

### Spring (April-June)
- Maple syrup season (Michigan UP, Wisconsin)
- CSA season begins (many states)
- Strawberries (Michigan, Illinois)

### Summer (June-August)
- Cherry season (Michigan, Wisconsin) - mid-July to mid-August
- Blueberry season (Michigan)
- Wild rice harvest begins (Minnesota) - August 15
- Fresh produce CSA peak season

### Fall (September-October)
- Apples (Minnesota, Michigan)
- Cranberries (Wisconsin) - harvest and tours in October
- Pumpkins and fall produce
- Rush Creek Reserve cheese (Wisconsin - fall production)

### Year-Round
- Dried cherries, blueberries
- Maple syrup products
- Cheese and dairy
- Frozen meats
- Wild rice
- Honey
- Wine
- Frozen fruits
- Cherry juice concentrate
