- Maine's top agricultural products: #1 Potatoes, #2 Milk, #3 Blueberries

- Maine is largest blueberry producer in the world
- Maine dairy plant codes (23-XX): HP Hood (23-03), Houlton Farms (23-20), Oakhurst (23-01), Smiling Hill (23-31)

### Massachusetts
- Cranberries are Commonwealth's #1 food crop

- Strong CSA network serving Greater Boston

### Regional Notes
- Many farms use USPS, FedEx for 2-5 day shipping

- Many farms offer SNAP/HIP acceptance for CSA shares
- Most CSA seasons run May/June through October/November
- Year-round CSAs typically offer root vegetables, storage crops, frozen/canned goods

## KEY FINDINGS

1. **Vermont dominates in**: Artisan cheese, maple syrup, organic dairy
2. **New York strengths**: Wine (Finger Lakes, Long Island), apples/cider (Hudson Valley), specialty cheese (Adirondacks, Finger Lakes)
3. **Maine specializes in**: Lobster/seafood, wild blueberries, potatoes (Aroostook), specialty foods
4. **Massachusetts excels at**: CSA networks, cranberries, immigrant farmer programs
5. **Rhode Island focus**: Oysters/shellfish, artisan cheese
6. **New Hampshire**: Organic dairy, farm-to-table CSAs
7. **Connecticut**: Historic cider mills, farm-direct programs

## VERIFIED SHIPPING CAPABILITIES

Most farms ship via:
- FedEx (2-3 day standard, overnight for perishables)
- USPS Priority Mail (flat rate boxes common for non-perishables)
- Specialized lobster/seafood shippers (overnight with gel packs)

Common policies:
- Free shipping thresholds: $75-$150
- Flat rate shipping: $7.95-$40 depending on product type
- Adult signature required for alcohol
- Temperature-controlled packaging for dairy/cheese/seafood
- Most ship to continental US; some to AK/HI; alcohol varies by state laws
