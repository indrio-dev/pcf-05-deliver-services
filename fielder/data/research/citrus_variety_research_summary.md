# Citrus Variety Trial Data Collection Summary

## Research Objective
Collect citrus variety trial data with complete Brix measurements (Location + Time + Brix) from university sources for major commercial cultivars.

## Data Collection Results

### Successfully Collected: 38 Complete Measurements

**Criteria Met:**
- ✅ Location (city/county/region)
- ✅ Time of harvest (month/season/date)
- ✅ Brix measurement value

### Key Findings by Cultivar

#### Cara Cara (Navel)
- **Brix Range**: 10.4 - 12.8 °Brix
- **Peak Sweetness**: January (12.8 °Brix)
- **Harvest Season**: November - January
- **Best Location**: Riverside, CA
- **Notes**: Mid-to-late season navel with consistent quality

#### Lane Late (Navel)
- **Brix Range**: 9.4 - 15.9 °Brix
- **Peak Sweetness**: March & June (15.9 °Brix)
- **Harvest Season**: February - June
- **Best Location**: Riverside, CA
- **Notes**: Ultra-late navel, holds well on tree, Brix increases through season

#### Powell (Navel)
- **Brix Range**: 12.15 - 15.95 °Brix
- **Peak Sweetness**: June (15.95 °Brix)
- **Harvest Season**: March - June
- **Best Location**: Riverside, CA
- **Notes**: Australian ultra-late selection, 3 weeks later than standard varieties

#### Fisher (Navel)
- **Brix Range**: 9.45 - 12.2 °Brix
- **Peak Sweetness**: Mid-November (12.2 °Brix at Riverside)
- **Harvest Season**: September - November
- **Best Location**: Riverside, CA
- **Notes**: Early-season navel, reaches maturity ahead of Washington

#### Fukumoto (Navel)
- **Brix Range**: 10.3 - 11.2 °Brix
- **Peak Sweetness**: October (11.2 °Brix)
- **Harvest Season**: September - October
- **Best Location**: Riverside, CA
- **Notes**: Very early navel, 3-4 weeks before Washington, distinctive red rind

#### Blood Oranges (IVIA Trial, Spain)
- **Tarocco Comune**: 15.03 °Brix (highest)
- **Moro**: 12.8 °Brix
- **Sanguinelli**: 12.03 °Brix
- **Tarocco Rosso**: 12.13 °Brix
- **Tarocco Messina**: 11.33 °Brix (lowest)
- **Harvest**: Physiological maturity, 2017-2018 seasons

### Cultivars NOT Fully Documented (Missing Complete Data)

#### Valencia Oranges
- **Status**: Qualitative data only from UF/IFAS trials
- **Findings**:
  - Valencia Late shows TSS around 12.56 °Brix (Florida)
  - Midknight Valencia: 11.6 °Brix average
  - Delta Valencia: Lower than other Valencias (no specific values)
  - SSC/TA ratios increased to over 25 by June in some trials
- **Issue**: Specific location + date + Brix combinations not published in accessible sources

#### Hamlin Oranges
- **Status**: Limited specific data
- **Findings**:
  - Brix/acid ratio: 13.01 to 20.28
  - Generally lower Brix than Valencia (requires blending for NFC juice)
  - Early-midseason cultivar (October-January harvest)
- **Issue**: Detailed trial data by location and harvest date not publicly available

#### Grapefruit Varieties
- **Ruby Red, Rio Red, Rio Star**:
  - Rio Star: 9-11 °Brix (Texas Rio Grande Valley)
  - Harvest: October - May (peak December)
  - Issue: No complete trial data with specific location + date + Brix

- **Flame, Marsh**:
  - Descriptive info only, no trial data found

## Data Format & Usage

**Output File**: `university_citrus_variety_brix_data.json`

**Structure**:
```json
{
  "cultivar_id": "string (snake_case)",
  "cultivar_name": "string (display name)",
  "location": "string (city, state/country)",
  "county": "string",
  "state": "string (state/country code)",
  "harvest_year": integer,
  "harvest_month": integer,
  "harvest_date": "YYYY-MM-DD",
  "brix": float,
  "acid": float (null if not available),
  "source": "string (institution)",
  "url": "string (source URL)",
  "notes": "string (context)"
}
```

**Ready for**: Neo4j ingestion, Fielder database integration, seasonal prediction models

## Key Insights

### Seasonal Brix Patterns
1. **Early Navels** (Oct-Nov): 9-11 °Brix (Fukumoto, Fisher)
2. **Mid-Season Navels** (Dec-Jan): 11-13 °Brix (Cara Cara, Washington)
3. **Late Navels** (Feb-Jun): 13-16 °Brix (Lane Late, Powell) - highest sweetness

### Geographic Variation
- **Riverside, CA**: Consistently higher Brix across varieties
- **Coachella Valley, CA**: Lower Brix (likely due to heat stress)
- **Lindcove, CA**: Moderate Brix, good for research baseline

### Blood Orange Quality
- **Tarocco Comune**: Highest quality (15.03 °Brix)
- **Moro**: Good balance (12.8 °Brix)
- **Commercial implication**: Tarocco varieties superior for fresh market

### Data Gaps
- **Florida Valencia & Hamlin**: Most critical gap for juice orange predictions
- **Texas Grapefruit**: Critical for winter grapefruit season modeling
- **Washington Navel**: Reference cultivar needs more complete data
