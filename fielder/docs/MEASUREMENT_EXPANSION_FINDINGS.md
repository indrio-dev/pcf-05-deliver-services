## Why So Little Data Found

### Major Obstacles

**1. Paywall Access (40% of sources)**
- ResearchGate: 403 Forbidden errors
- ScienceDirect: Paywall/access restricted
- ASHS journals: Full articles blocked
- University PDFs: Binary/unreadable via web fetch

**2. Incomplete Public Reporting (50% of sources)**
- Universities mention trials but don't publish Brix values
- Qualitative descriptions ("excellent quality") vs quantitative data
- Location vague ("Texas", "Florida" vs specific counties)
- Timing seasonal ("summer") vs dated ("July 15")

**3. Proprietary Breeding Programs (10% of sources)**
- Research papers use codes (PF 24-C) not cultivar names
- Breeding selections not publicly disclosed
- Brix data exists but cultivar identity hidden

### Examples of Frustration

**UGA Fort Valley (Peach belt):**
- Extensive peach research program exists
- Extension materials mention variety trials
- **NO Brix measurements in publicly indexed publications**

**Texas A&M Citrus Center:**
- Rio Star, Rio Red are Texas varieties
- Research papers exist (found references)
- **Actual data tables behind paywalls or not web-accessible**

**UF/IFAS CREC:**
- Valencia, Hamlin rootstock trials mentioned on website
- "Data available" but not in searchable format
- **Need to request raw trial reports directly**

## What We Learned

### Web Search Limitations

**What works:**
‚úÖ Finding that trials exist
‚úÖ Identifying researchers and programs
‚úÖ Getting general Brix ranges
‚úÖ Understanding harvest seasons

**What doesn't work:**
‚ùå Getting complete Location + Time + Brix datasets
‚ùå Accessing paywalled publications
‚ùå Extracting data from university PDF reports
‚ùå Finding cultivar-specific measurements vs general ranges

### Data Access Reality

**University trial data EXISTS but is:**
- Published in paywalled journals (need institutional access)
- Stored in extension databases (need direct request)
- Reported in annual summaries (need to request from researchers)
- Presented at conferences (proceedings not online)

**To get complete datasets, need:**
- Direct email contact with researchers
- Request for raw trial data or annual reports
- Institutional access to journal databases
- OR partnerships with universities for data sharing

### What Worked

- **Why it worked:** Extension outreach format, not research paper

**2. PMC Open Access (Blueberries):**

- Complete data tables with all parameters
- **Why it worked:** Open access journal, European publication

**3. UC Riverside Citrus Collection (Navels - previous work):**
- Variety database with measurement data
- Complete historical records
- **Why it worked:** Public database, institutional commitment to data sharing

**4. MSU Extension (Apples - previous work):**
- Weekly maturity reports published online
- Complete data for commercial orchards
- **Why it worked:** Extension service mission = public data sharing
