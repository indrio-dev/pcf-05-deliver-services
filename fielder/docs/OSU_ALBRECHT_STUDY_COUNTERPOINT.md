# OSU Albrecht Study: The Critical Counterpoint

**Source:** Ohio State University soil balancing research (2015-2019, 4 years)
**Researchers:** Steve Coleman (OSU soil specialist), Will Auster-Hall (ARS)
**Finding:** **NO yield effects from Ca:Mg ratio manipulation**

**This CHALLENGES the Albrecht method claims!**

---

## What OSU Did (Rigorous Test)

**Experimental design:**
```
Two sites:
- Worcester: Silt loam, CEC 10-15 (moderate - Albrecht's sweet spot!)
- Herzl: Hoytville clay, CEC 25+ (high)

Treatments (aggressive!):
- Gypsum: Up to 6,500 lbs/acre over 4 years
- High-cal lime
- Dolomitic lime (high Mg)
- Epsom salts (pure Mg)
- Sulfur (to separate pH from Ca effects)

Goal: Create MAXIMUM separation in Ca:Mg ratios
Result: Successfully changed ratios (proof of concept worked)
```

**What they measured:**
```
- Ca:Mg ratios (successfully manipulated)
- Soil pH (successfully changed with lime)
- Crop tissue nutrients
- Soil health indicators (3 biological tests)
- Soil physical properties (penetration, infiltration)
- Crop yields (corn, soy, small grain)
```

---

## The SHOCKING Results

**After 4 years, aggressive treatments:**

### ❌ NO Yield Effects
```
Corn yield: No difference
Soybean yield: No difference
Small grain yield: No difference

Applied 6,500 lbs gypsum/acre
Changed Ca:Mg from 4.5:1 to 8:1
NO yield response!
```

### ❌ NO Soil Health Effects
```
Tested 3 biological indicators
High tillage organic system
NO differences in any soil health metrics
```

### ❌ NO Crop Nutrient Effects
```
Tissue Ca: Increased where Ca added (obvious)
Tissue Mg: Increased where Mg added (obvious)
But: All above sufficiency levels
N, P, K: NO differences
Micronutrients: NO differences (except pH effects)

No "knock-on" effects from Ca:Mg balance
```

### ⚠️ MINIMAL Physical Property Effects
```
Penetration resistance:
- July: No difference
- October: Slight difference (Mg increased resistance)
- Marginal effect at best

Infiltration:
- July: No difference
- October: Possible gypsum benefit (high error bars)
- Not statistically significant
```

**Conclusion from OSU:**
> "No evidence to suggest soil balancing affects yield or crop quality in these systems"
